⚡ AC Power Calculation (400 V / 230 V)

Active & Apparent Power

P = n × Pnom × load / η Active power with simultaneity factor, load in % and efficiency η
S = P / cos φ Apparent power (without VFD)
S = P / 0.90 Apparent power (with VFD, assumed total power factor ≈ 0.90)

Current Calculation

I = S × 1000 / (U × √3) Phase current, 3-phase (S in kVA, U in V)
I = S × 1000 / U Current, single-phase (S in kVA, U in V)

Fuse Sizing

Ifuse = Ipeak × (1 + margin%) Peak current with safety margin, rounded up to standard fuse rating
PFtotal = Ptotal / Stotal Total power factor for the entire installation

🔋 24 V DC — Direct Current Calculations

Power & Current

P = U × I Power, direct current (Ohm's law)
Itotal = Σ (qty × Iunit) Total load current summed from all consumers

Cable Voltage Drop

Rcable = ρ × L / A Cable resistance (ρ = resistivity, L = length, A = cross-section area)
ΔU = 2 × I × Rcable Voltage drop forward + return in cable
ΔU% = (ΔU / Usupply) × 100 Voltage drop as percentage of supply voltage
Uload = Usupply − ΔU Voltage at load after voltage drop

Fuse Selection (DC)

Ifuse = Itotal × (1 + margin%) Selected fuse with safety margin (typically 20%)

🔄 VFD — Harmonics & Power Factor

Total Power Factor

λ = cos φ × 1 / √(1 + THDi²) Total power factor = displacement × distortion factor
VFD approx: 0.97 × 1/√(1 + 0.40²) ≈ 0.90 Typical cos φ ≈ 0.97 and THDi ≈ 40% gives λ ≈ 0.90

Current incl. Harmonics

IRMS = I₁ × √(1 + THDi²) True RMS current including harmonic distortion
Ih = √(IRMS² − I₁²) Total harmonic current
I₁ = P / (√3 × U × cos φ × η) Theoretical fundamental current, 3-phase motor

Line Reactor Inductance

L = (uk × Uphase) / (2π × f × I) × 1000 Line reactor inductance in mH (uk = 3–4%)

🌡️ Power Loss & Enclosure Cooling

Loss Calculation

Ptotal = Σ (qty × Ploss/unit) Total power loss inside the enclosure
Pdesign = Ptotal × derating% Design power loss with derating factor (typically 80%)

Thermal Sizing

ΔT = Tmax − Tambient Temperature difference between enclosure and ambient
A = 2(d×h) + 2(w×h) + w×d Effective cooling surface (sides + back + top, excl. bottom)
Pnatural = k × A × ΔT Dissipated power via natural convection (k ≈ 5.5 W/(m²·K))
Arequired = P / (k × ΔT) Required cooling surface with natural convection only

📘 Fundamentals — Ohm's Law & Power Triangle

Ohm's Law

U = R × I Voltage = Resistance × Current
I = U / R Current = Voltage / Resistance
R = U / I Resistance = Voltage / Current

Power Triangle (DC & pure AC)

P = U × I Power = Voltage × Current
P = I² × R Power = Current² × Resistance
P = U² / R Power = Voltage² / Resistance

Power Triangle (AC 3-phase)

P = √3 × U × I × cos φ Active power, 3-phase
S = √3 × U × I Apparent power, 3-phase
Q = √3 × U × I × sin φ Reactive power, 3-phase
S² = P² + Q² Relationship between S, P and Q
